The Matchup score blends the opponent's DvP rank (60%) with the player's own PFF grade (40%) — an 88+ grade is treated as matchup proof and floors at 7.0 no matter the defense. Defensive explosiveness allowed (CFBD, garbage time excluded) then adjusts: a leaky-quartile defense adds up to +1.25 (more when the player is himself explosive — YPRR 2.0+ for WRs, 5.5+ per carry for RBs), a stingy-quartile defense subtracts, gently for elite grades. Hover any score for its components. Starters only uses the STARTER marks from your workbook when the export carries them; otherwise it keeps each team's top of the depth chart by the workbook's usage column — the QB1, top-2 RBs, and top-3 WRs — which tracks who started the previous week; toggle it off for the full pool. Detail column: QBs show the opponent's completion % allowed, WRs the opponent's catch rate allowed to receivers, RBs the trench battle — the offense's line yards created per rush against the defense's line yards conceded, with the differential (positive favors the runner). Matchup score 1-10 from the opponent's defense-vs-position rank across all FBS: 10 = the defense that allows the most fantasy points to this position, 1 = the stingiest. DvP Rank reads "#120 of 135" — the higher the number, the softer the defense. Players whose opponent isn't set (or is FCS without data) show a dash.
